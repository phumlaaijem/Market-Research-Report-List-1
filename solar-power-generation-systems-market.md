<p><p>Solar power generation systems are classified into centralized and decentralized PV power plants. Centralized PV power plants typically involve large-scale installations that generate significant electricity from a single location, feeding energy into the grid. They benefit from economies of scale but require substantial land and investment. In contrast, decentralized PV power plants consist of smaller, distributed systems, often installed on rooftops or local sites, allowing for localized energy production. This model enhances energy accessibility and reduces transmission losses while promoting energy independence.</p></p>
