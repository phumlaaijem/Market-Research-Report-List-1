<p><p>The microemulsions market is categorized into four main types: anionic, non-ionic, cationic, and zwitterionic. Anionic microemulsions carry a negative charge and are often used in applications requiring emulsification stability. Non-ionic microemulsions lack an ionic charge, providing versatility and compatibility across various systems. Cationic microemulsions possess a positive charge, which enhances their effectiveness in surfactant formulations. Zwitterionic microemulsions contain both positive and negative charges, offering unique stability and compatibility for specialized applications. Each type serves distinct industrial needs.</p></p>
